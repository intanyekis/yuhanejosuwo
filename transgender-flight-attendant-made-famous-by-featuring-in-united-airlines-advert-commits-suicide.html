Transgender flight attendant made famous by featuring in United Airlines advert commits suicide

 Posted on June 3, 2024  |  1 minutes  |  168 words  |  Martina Birk

The tragic loss of a transgender flight attendant who rose to prominence as part of a United Airlines campaign on transgender awareness has been reported. Kayleigh Scott, 25, was discovered deceased at her residence in Denver, Colorado, on March 20th. Prior to her passing, she posted a poignant message on her social media accounts expressing remorse for letting down those she cared about. Her final words paid tribute to a friend who had taken her own life in 2016.

Friends initially responded with support and offers of assistance, and some alerted authorities about the concerning post. However, several hours later, Kayleigh’s sister Ashley confirmed her passing, thanking everyone for their concern and love. The loss of Kayleigh Scott has deeply impacted those who knew and cared for her.
