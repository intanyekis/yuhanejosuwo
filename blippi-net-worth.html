Blippi's YouTube Channels

As an early adopter to YouTube (starting in 2014), Blippi uploaded fun, engaging educational videos with playful music, which worked well with toddlers' limited attention spans.

His energetic persona and colorful exploration of topics like the garbage truck, fire station, jungle gym, and excavation work delighted young viewers.

With daily view counts rapidly expanding, Blippi optimized thumbnails and titles to boost his ranking, resulting in over 19 million subscribers and nearly 16 billion total video views.

In addition to Blippi's main channel ("Blippi - Educational Videos for Kids"), the YouTuber maintains another lucrative channel on the platform, Blippi Toys (@blippitoys).

Blippi Toys has over eleven million subscribers and nearly twelve billion views, and it serves as a massive second income stream.

Blippi built a massive, dedicated audience, which became a lucrative path to diversifying his brand across media platforms, merchandise, and live entertainment events.

Blippi Optimized His Videos For Higher View Counts

With the core knowledge that a toddler's attention is fleeting, Blippi designed every aspect of his videos for maximum engagement.

He puts on a lively performance with an active stage personality, simple language, colorful images, lighthearted music, a rapid interchange of scenes, and physical humor. Blippi uses search-optimized titles as well as eye-catching thumbnails.

Blippi videos are carefully edited to suit the short attention spans of young viewers, with an eye toward critical educational concepts.

The formula gets more and more data-informed through continual testing, examining watch times, and assessing viewership data, and Blippi's videos provide a good balance of information and entertainment to his growing audience.

Blippi's Loyal Fan Base

Part of Blippi's appeal is his bubbly, good-natured personality that evokes and stimulates a toddler's sense of fun.

With the release of new videos on a regular schedule, Blippi became familiar with and established trust in young fans who eagerly waited to go along on virtual trips every week.

Today, small children instantly recognize his trademark orange hat and bowtie, colorful shirts, and signature dance moves. Parents, too, liked his educational lessons, which kept their children amused.

These emotional connections spawned a loyal cadre of repeat viewers as Blippi's "generation" matured. The preschool-aged fan base of Blippi generates more than 200 million monthly views between channels.

Blippi Merchandise

Building upon the devotion of his youthful audience as his channel raced up the YouTube charts, Blippi started to brand merchandise.

But his consumer goods move the spirit of his playful image beyond video: toys, clothes, books, toothbrushes, and vitamins are but a few Blippi branded pieces of merchandise (or "merch.")

Walmart and Target are stacked with shelves of Blippi figurines, play sets, and role-play items following a significant licensing deal with toy-maker Playmates.

Other alliances yielded cereals, birthday party paraphernalia carrying Blippi's image, and a steady raft of cash. Parents have familiar buying options for Blippi merchandise, which means repeated exposure to the brand for children.

Expanded to New Platforms

When Blippi saw the potential to expand his viewership of children further, he started branching out from YouTube in 2017.

Streaming services such as The Roku Channel, Amazon Prime Video, and Tubi TV started to offer Blippi videos and games. In 2014, the Blippi app introduced subscriptions for premium content, recording more than 60 million downloads by 2023.

He was introduced to network television through a Blippi TV show produced by Genius Brands, and in 2018, CNN reported:

"Blippi's [Stevin] John works with some brands but says he is picky because he feels protective of his young viewers. Instead, the [then] 30-year-old's biggest projects are around licensing. His episodes are now on Amazon and Roku, and he's working on an educational app. There's also a deal with K-Swiss to sell a replica of Blippi's shoes in Footlocker at the end of [2018], and another for Blippi-branded push bikes, scooters and other outdoor gear for kids from Westridge."

Getting the word to kids on mobile devices, smart TVs, and cable programming increased total viewership. It also opened up additional channels for monetizing content with subscriber fees and licensing royalties.

Blippi Created A Top-Selling App

Leveraging his popularity as a YouTube personality in 2017, Blippi released an educational app that quickly became one of the most downloaded.

Boasting over 50 million downloads, the Blippi app provides interactive learning with new videos and games updated weekly.

The app's subscription costs $7.99 a month or $59.99 annually, where premium content and offline viewing – major revenue streams – are unlocked.

Blippi used stickers, coloring pages, and singalongs to fortify his brand appeal and encourage early media assimilation. This mobile platform lets him promote his latest videos, events, and products quickly and conveniently to his fans.

By expanding to the world of apps, Blippi's brand established a stronger personal relationship with his faithful audience.

Blippi's Partnerships With Major Companies

Expanding his brand was essential to Blippi, and John worked with massive corporations in toys, gaming, entertainment media, and consumer products.

In 2019, a master toy deal with Playmates led to the widespread distribution of figurines, play sets, and role-playing toys based on Blippi. Gaming publisher Outright Games produced Blippi video games.

Media giant Moonbug acquired the rights to produce new content and manage Blippi licensing globally.

Other major partners include brands such as Jazwares, Kiloo, Warner Music, and Universal Kids.

These high-profile connections created a platform of infrastructure and marketing dollars to allow Blippi expand his reach into new types of merchandise, live shows, and more.

Blippi Tours

Banking on his popularity in 2019, Blippi took his act to stages nationwide for his first concert tour — Blippi Live!

The show provided families with singing and dancing, costumed characters, and meet-and-greets.

With five separate tours around North America, Blippi brought millions of fans the interactive experience they missed on YouTube.

By 2022, the tours would bring in more than $20 million at the box office, helping him continue to monetize his family-friendly brand – and adding a significant share to Blippi's net worth.

Fans (and parents of fans) are likely eagerly awaiting Blippi's 2024 tour, Blippi: The Wonderful World Tour; tickets for a February 2024 Blippi show in Florida ranged from $25 to $69.50 in late 2023.

Blippi Launched A TV Show

In May 2018, the children's series Blippi premiered on Universal Kids, bringing his charismatic YouTube persona to cable television.

Genius Brands and Pocket produced the project, an educational program featuring animations and live-action segments with a young cast.

As one of the channel's top-rated shows, Blippi unlocked additional revenue through advertiser sponsorships, corporate partnerships, merchandise sales, and distribution fees.

The TV series greatly expanded the Blippi brand's visibility beyond streaming platforms, and into millions of homes nationwide.

Blippi's Net Worth (And Moonbug Deal)

On July 30th 2020, entertainment news publication Variety reported that Blippi had been acquired by Moonbug Entertainment, as part of a larger deal involving the similar brand Cocomelon:

"[In addition to Cocomelon,] Moonbug also said it acquired Blippi, a live-action educational show on YouTube that generates more than half a billion average monthly views that feature the eponymous character, created by Stevin John. Terms of the deals were not disclosed."

As indicated, the terms of the deal were not disclosed, one of many factors making Blippi's net worth difficult to calculate.

What Is Blippi's Net Worth?

Myriad websites claim Blippi's net worth is $140 million, a figure sometimes sourced to a 2020 Forbes blogger's list of highest-paid YouTubers in that year.

However, the list makes no such claim, nor does it include any specific figure from which $140 could reasonably be extrapolated.

It described John as a "newcomer" to the list in 2020, and provided three figures for that specific year:

Earnings: $17 million

Views: 8.2 billion

Subscribers: 27.4 million

Across the web in 2024, Blippi's net worth has been estimated at $16 million, $20 million, $40 million, $75 million, $90 million, and the unsubstantiated $140 million sum falsely sourced to "Forbes' Richest."

More than one source claims Blippi's net worth is $140 million, and the figure is routinely attributed to Forbes.

However, a search of Forbes' website showed Stevin John's Blippi had only been referenced in two articles in recent years, one of which is linked above.

The second article was published in January 2022, but neither Blippi's net worth nor his channel were visibly mentioned in the piece.

The most credible range of figures we found pertaining to Blippi's net worth were on SocialBlade's pages for his two major YouTube channels, Blippi and Blippi Toys.

According to the database, John could have earned between $422,900,000 and $6.8 million per year through his main channel, and an additional $269,900,000 to $4.3 million for Blippi Toys, not counting other revenue streams – but Moonbug's acquisition of the rights to Blippi further clouds such a calculation.

Blippi's net worth is likely higher than the annual earnings of $17 million reported by Forbes in 2020, and likely not as high as the $140 million estimate – misleadingly sourced to the same 2020 article.
