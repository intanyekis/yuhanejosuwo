Larry Fitzgerald Biography

An American football player Larry Fitzgerald is a wide receiver for National Football League (NFL)’s Arizona Cardinals. He plays alongside teammates David Johnson, Patrick Peterson, Chase Edmonds, and many others.

Early Life and Education

Larry Fitzgerald was born as Larry Darnell Fitzgerald Jr on 31st August of 1983 in Minneapolis, Minnesota. He was raised by Larry Fitzgerald Sr. and Carol Fitzgerald. His father, Larry Fitzgerald Sr. is a sportswriter at Minnesota Spokesman-Records.

About his mother, Carol Fitzgerald died in 2003 due to a brain hemorrhage while she was getting treatment for breast cancer. In addition, he grew up with a younger brother named Marcus Fitzgerald who is also a football player. 

During his High school, Fitzgerald started playing football at Richfield (MN) Academy of Holy Angels. Further, he joined the University of Pittsburgh and played for the Pittsburgh Panthers football team. In his college years from 2002-2003, he was known as one of the best wide receivers. 

Moreover, in the history of the Pittsburgh Panthers football team, Fitzgerald is the ninth player to receive the honor of having his #1 jersey retired by the university.
