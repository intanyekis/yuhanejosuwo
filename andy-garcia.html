Andy García is a Cuban-born American actor and director, who rose to prominence in the 1980s with films like The Untouchables and The Godfather: Part III. Although he has achieved popularity for his roles in cult gangster movies, his charming presence coupled with his onscreen elegance made him one of the most sought-after leading men in the film industry. García has become a well-established name in Hollywood but he is equally reputed for being very guarded in his personal life and usually prefers to stay out of the limelight. His long-standing career received a boost in 2018 with all 4 of his films receiving good reviews in that year.

Education

Andy studied at the Miami Beach Senior High School. He was involved in his school’s basketball team but had to quit due to a bout of mononucleosis. During his senior year, he took acting lessons with drama teacher Jay W. Jensen, who has also coached the likes of Mickey Rourke, Brett Ratner, etc.

He went on to attend and complete his graduation from Florida International University, located in Miami.

    Andy García Facts

  • Andy was 2 years old when Fidel Castro came into power in Cuba in 1959. Following the Bay of Pigs Invasion in 1961, Andy’s father relocated his wife and 3 kids, including 5-year-old Andy, to Miami in Florida. After moving to the US, the García family launched a perfume company which has become a successful million-dollar business venture over time.
  • When he was born, he had remnants of an undeveloped conjoined twin attached to him by his shoulder. The twin was the size of a grapefruit and had to be surgically removed. All that’s left is a scar from that operation.
  • He is good friends with fellow actor Mickey Rourke. Rourke was his baseball coach while studying at Miami Beach Senior High School.
  • García was included in Empire Magazine‘s 1995 issue as one of the ‘100 Sexiest Stars in film history’ at the 65th spot.
  • He has never done n#de scenes in films. He refused to oblige when asked to appear shirtless for an audition and famously walked out.
  • Winning the role of Vincent Mancini in Francis Ford Coppola‘s third installment in The Godfather series put him on the map as an actor. The role was one of the most coveted parts of the decade with many established actors like Val Kilmer, Robert De Niro, Alec Baldwin, Charlie Sheen, and Vincent Spano campaigning for the part.
  • Andy’s niece Jackie García was engaged to footballer Sean Taylor. Sean, a Washington Redskins safety, was shot by intruders in his house on November 26, 2007. Although Jackie and their 18-month-old daughter were unharmed in the incident, her fiancé Taylor succumbed to his injuries and died the next day.
  • In recent years, he has expressed his interest to return to his home country of Cuba. But, he has clarified that he is opposed to the current authoritarian regime of the Castro family.
  • García directed the 2005 film The Lost City, written by Cuban writer Guillermo Cabrera Infante. The film took 16 years to make and is a love song to his Cuban culture and its heritage.
